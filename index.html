Mission Overview — Artemis II

Artemis II marks the first crewed flight around the Moon since Apollo 17 (1972). The four-person crew will perform a free-return trajectory lunar flyby during April 6–7, 2026, providing the first human observations of the Moon from cislunar space in over 50 years.

Guide to the Artemis II Lunar Targeting Plan
What is the LTP?

The Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) provides the timeline and science and operational objectives for the Artemis II crew to execute during the lunar flyby. It was developed by the Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) Team, a subgroup of the Artemis II Lunar Science Team.

Flyby Key Events

Start: April 6, 20:45 GMT — Flyby activities begin
Earthset: April 6, 22:45 GMT — Earth disappears behind Moon
Closest Approach: April 6, 23:02 GMT
Earthrise: April 6, 23:25 GMT — Earth reappears
Eclipse: ~1 hour total solar eclipse from Orion
End: April 7, 01:20 GMT

Observation Methods

Feature ID: Identify feature type/name, confirm target, location, observation style
Geometry: Describe size, shape, boundary preservation
Color & Shadow: Color tone, albedo, shadows/illumination
Structure & Texture: Structures, slopes, texture
Geologic Relations: Association, contact habit, contact relationship

Crew Autonomy

The Artemis II astronauts have the autonomy to make changes according to mission needs and their judgment. The exact time, nature, and completeness of their science activities and mission events is subject to change. Crew Choice targets allow free scientific exploration.

Camera Focal Lengths

Unaided Eye: Full Moon overview, large-scale features, terminator regions
135mm: Regional surveys, basin-scale features, highland/mare boundaries
200mm: Individual craters, basin rings, color variations
400mm: High-resolution crater interiors, ray systems, small-scale features
